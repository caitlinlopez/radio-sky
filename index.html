The Crab Nebula
The Crab Nebula in the constellation Taurus is the remnant of a bright supernova recorded by Chinese astronomers in 1054. At the center of the nebula lies the Crab pulsar (link to wiki), a compact neutron star spinning ~30 times per second. The neutron star formed at the time of the explosion due to the gravitational collapse of the center of the star that was destroyed in the supernova explosion. It is incredibly dense containing 1.4 times the mass of the Sun within a diameter of just ~20 km. A single teaspoon of material would weigh a billion tons! Both the nebula and the pulsar are two of the most well-studied objects in astrophysics.
